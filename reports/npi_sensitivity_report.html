NPI Formula Sensitivity Analysis

81 permutations across 4 parameters | 80 converged, 1 diverged | 15 bubble teams | February 27, 2026 at 1:58 PM

Why do some parameter combinations diverge?

The NPI formula calculates each team's NPI iteratively (until the system converges). Each game's NPI depends on the opponent's NPI from the previous iteration. For a quality win, the effective coefficient on opponent NPI is:

game_npi = 100*RW + opp_npi * SOS_WEIGHT + (opp_npi - threshold) * QBM

A team's final NPI averages across all games. Losses contribute only opp_npi * SOS_WEIGHT (coefficient < 1), which dampens. But quality wins contribute opp_npi * (SOS_WEIGHT + QBM). When enough of a team's games are quality wins and SOS_WEIGHT + QBM is large, the dampening from losses is insufficient and NPIs grow without bound across iterations.

The current formula (SOS=0.80, QBM=0.667) converges because the average across wins and losses keeps the effective multiplier below 1. Higher QBM with lower thresholds (more games qualify as quality wins) tips the balance toward divergence.
